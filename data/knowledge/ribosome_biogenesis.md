# Ribosome Biogenesis and rRNA Processing

## Overview
Ribosome biogenesis is a complex cellular process involving the synthesis and assembly of ribosomal RNA (rRNA) and ribosomal proteins into functional ribosomal subunits. This process occurs primarily in the nucleolus and requires hundreds of assembly factors.

## Key Pathways and Processes

### Pre-rRNA Processing
The 40S ribosomal subunit requires extensive processing of the 18S rRNA precursor. Key genes involved include:
- **BMS1**: GTPase essential for 40S ribosome synthesis and pre-rRNA processing
- **BYSL**: Required for processing of 20S pre-rRNA precursor to mature 18S rRNA
- **NOB1**: Endonuclease that cleaves pre-rRNA at site D to generate mature 18S rRNA
- **RRP12**: Required for maturation of both 40S and 60S ribosomal subunits

### Small Subunit (SSU) Processome
The SSU processome is a large ribonucleoprotein complex required for processing of 18S rRNA:
- **AATF**: Component of the SSU processome, involved in first cleavage step
- **C1orf131** (UBTD1): Part of the SSU processome complex
- **ESF1**: Nucleolar protein required for pre-rRNA processing

### RNA Helicases in Ribosome Biogenesis
ATP-dependent RNA helicases are critical for rRNA folding and remodeling:
- **DDX47**: DEAD-box helicase involved in 40S ribosome maturation
- **DDX52**: DEAD-box helicase required for early steps of 60S ribosome assembly
- **DDX56**: Nucleolar DEAD-box helicase involved in ribosome biogenesis

### Ribosome Assembly Factors
- **NOP14**: Nucleolar protein involved in 60S ribosome subunit biogenesis
- **NOP16**: Nucleolar protein required for 60S ribosome maturation
- **WDR43**: WD repeat protein involved in 18S rRNA processing

### Methylation and Modification
- **DIMT1**: Methyltransferase that modifies 18S rRNA, essential for ribosome function
- **WBSCR22**: Involved in rRNA processing and ribosome biogenesis

## Novel Connections

### Chromatin Remodeling and Ribosome Biogenesis
Recent studies suggest unexpected links between chromatin remodeling complexes and ribosome biogenesis:
- **INO80**: While primarily known as a chromatin remodeler, INO80 may regulate rDNA transcription
- The INO80 complex has been implicated in maintaining nucleolar structure

### Metabolic Regulation
- **HK2** (Hexokinase 2): Glycolytic enzyme; potential link to ribosome biogenesis through energy sensing
- Ribosome production is highly energy-dependent and may be coupled to glycolytic flux

### Kinase Regulation
- **DYRK1A**: Dual-specificity kinase; may phosphorylate ribosome biogenesis factors
- DYRK1A has been linked to nucleolar function in some contexts

## Quality Control
- **NEPRO**: Nucleolar protein, potential role in ribosome quality control
- **ZCCHC9**: Zinc finger protein, function in ribosome biogenesis unclear

## Clinical Relevance
Defects in ribosome biogenesis genes cause ribosomopathies, including:
- Diamond-Blackfan anemia
- Treacher Collins syndrome
- Shwachman-Diamond syndrome

Dysregulated ribosome biogenesis is also a hallmark of cancer cells, which require high protein synthesis rates.
