# N6-Methyladenosine (m6A) mRNA Modification

## Overview
N6-methyladenosine (m6A) is the most abundant internal modification of eukaryotic mRNA. It regulates mRNA stability, translation, splicing, and localization. The m6A modification is dynamically installed by "writers," removed by "erasers," and recognized by "readers."

## m6A Writers (Methyltransferases)

### METTL3/METTL14 Complex
- **METTL3**: Catalytic methyltransferase subunit
- **METTL14**: Structural and RNA-binding subunit
- **WTAP**: Regulatory subunit required for complex localization
- **RBM15/RBM15B**: Recruit the complex to specific mRNA targets
- **VIRMA** (KIAA1429): Guide the complex to 3' UTRs

### METTL16
- Independent m6A writer targeting U6 snRNA and MAT2A mRNA

## m6A Erasers (Demethylases)

### FTO and ALKBH5
- **FTO**: Fat mass and obesity-associated protein, first identified m6A demethylase
- **ALKBH5**: AlkB homolog 5, m6A demethylase enriched in the nucleus

## m6A Readers (Binding Proteins)

### YTH Domain Family
- **YTHDF1**: Enhances mRNA translation efficiency
- **YTHDF2**: Promotes mRNA decay by recruiting deadenylase complexes
- **YTHDF3**: Cooperates with YTHDF1 and YTHDF2
- **YTHDC1**: Nuclear reader affecting splicing and export
- **YTHDC2**: Enhances translation and mRNA decay in germline cells

### IGF2BP Family
- **IGF2BP1/2/3**: Promote mRNA stability and translation
- Recognize m6A in GG(m6A)C motifs

### HNRNP Proteins with m6A Interactions
- **HNRNPC**: Binds to m6A-modified RNA and regulates splicing
- **HNRNPA2B1**: m6A reader affecting pri-miRNA processing
- **HNRNPD** (AUF1): RNA-binding protein with context-dependent roles
  - While primarily known for binding AU-rich elements (AREs), HNRNPD can interact with m6A-modified transcripts
  - Regulates mRNA stability and translation
  - Emerging evidence suggests HNRNPD cooperates with m6A machinery in specific contexts
  - May influence decay of m6A-modified mRNAs

## m6A-Regulated Processes

### mRNA Stability
- m6A-marked mRNAs are targeted for degradation by YTHDF2
- IGF2BP proteins protect m6A-RNAs from decay

### Translation Regulation
- YTHDF1 enhances translation of m6A-modified mRNAs
- m6A near stop codons affects ribosome dynamics

### RNA Splicing
- YTHDC1 recruits splicing factors to m6A sites
- m6A regulates alternative splicing and exon inclusion

### RNA Export and Localization
- YTHDC1 mediates nuclear export of m6A-modified mRNAs
- m6A affects mRNA localization in polarized cells and neurons

## m6A in Development and Differentiation

### Stem Cell Biology
- m6A regulates pluripotency factors (e.g., NANOG, SOX2)
- METTL3 is required for ES cell differentiation

### Neuronal Function
- m6A affects neuronal mRNA localization and local translation
- Critical for learning and memory

### Immune Responses
- m6A regulates cytokine mRNA stability
- Affects T cell homeostasis and activation

## m6A in Cancer

### Oncogenic Roles
- m6A writers (METTL3/14) promote oncogene expression (MYC, BCL2)
- FTO (eraser) can enhance oncogenic mRNA translation in AML

### Tumor Suppressive Roles
- ALKBH5 loss promotes cancer in some contexts
- m6A pathway mutations found in diverse cancers

### Therapeutic Potential
- Small molecule inhibitors of METTL3/14
- Targeting m6A readers to modulate mRNA fate

## RNA Binding Proteins and m6A Cross-talk

### AU-Rich Element Binding Proteins
- **HuR** (ELAVL1): Competes with YTHDF2 for mRNA binding
- **TTP** (ZFP36): Cooperates with m6A-mediated decay

### SR Proteins
- **SRSF3/SRSF10**: Interact with m6A-modified exons
- Influence splice site selection

## Clinical Relevance
m6A dysregulation is implicated in cancer, neurological disorders, obesity, and viral infections. Targeting the m6A pathway represents a novel therapeutic approach for modulating RNA metabolism.
