# mTOR Signaling Pathway

## Overview
The mechanistic target of rapamycin (mTOR) pathway is a central regulator of cell growth, proliferation, metabolism, and survival in response to nutrients, growth factors, and cellular energy status.

## Core mTOR Complexes

### mTORC1 (mTOR Complex 1)
**Core Components:**
- **MTOR**: Serine/threonine kinase, catalytic subunit
- **RPTOR** (Raptor): Regulatory-associated protein, substrate recruitment
- **MLST8** (mLST8/GβL): Stabilizes mTOR kinase domain
- **DEPTOR**: Negative regulator of mTORC1

**Upstream Activators:**
- **RHEB**: Ras homolog enriched in brain, direct mTORC1 activator (GTP-bound form)
- **PDPK1**: Phosphoinositide-dependent kinase-1, activates AKT which inhibits TSC complex

**Functions:**
- Protein synthesis via S6K and 4E-BP phosphorylation
- Lipid synthesis
- Nucleotide synthesis
- Autophagy inhibition

### mTORC2 (mTOR Complex 2)
**Core Components:**
- **MTOR**: Catalytic subunit (shared with mTORC1)
- **RICTOR**: Rapamycin-insensitive companion of mTOR, defines mTORC2
- **MLST8**: Shared with mTORC1
- **MAPKAP1** (SIN1): Stress-activated protein kinase-interacting protein
- **PRR5/PRR5L**: Proline-rich protein 5

**Functions:**
- AKT phosphorylation at Ser473 (full activation)
- Cytoskeletal organization
- Cell survival

## Regulatory Complexes

### GATOR1 Complex (mTORC1 Inhibitor)
Inhibits mTORC1 in response to amino acid depletion:
- **DEPDC5**: DEP domain containing 5
- **NPRL2**: Nitrogen permease regulator-like 2
- **NPRL3**: Nitrogen permease regulator-like 3

### GATOR2 Complex (mTORC1 Activator)
Inhibits GATOR1, thus activating mTORC1:
- **WDR24**: WD repeat domain 24
- **WDR59**: WD repeat domain 59
- **SEH1L**: SEH1-like nucleoporin
- **SEC13**: SEC13 homolog, nuclear pore complex component

### KICSTOR Complex
Recruits GATOR1 to lysosomes:
- **KPTN**: Kaptin
- **ITFG2**: Integrin alpha FG-GAP repeat containing 2
- **C12orf66**: Chromosome 12 open reading frame 66
- **SZT2**: Seizure threshold 2

## Amino Acid Sensing
- **Ragulator complex**: Recruits mTORC1 to lysosomal surface
- **Rag GTPases**: Sense amino acid availability
- **Sestrin2**: Leucine sensor that regulates GATOR2

## Novel Connections and Emerging Roles

### TRAPP Complexes and mTOR
The TRAPP (transport protein particle) complexes are Golgi-associated vesicle tethering factors with emerging links to mTOR:
- **TRAPPC3**: TRAPP complex component, may regulate mTOR localization
- **TRAPPC8**: Large TRAPP subunit, potential role in mTOR trafficking to lysosomes
- **TRAPPC11**: TRAPP component, mutations cause limb-girdle muscular dystrophy

Recent evidence suggests TRAPP complexes may regulate mTORC1 localization and activity through vesicular trafficking mechanisms.

### Bicarbonate Transport and pH Sensing
- **SLC4A7** (NBCn1): Sodium bicarbonate cotransporter, regulates intracellular pH
- mTORC1 activity is pH-sensitive; SLC4A7 may modulate mTOR through pH homeostasis
- Emerging role in metabolic sensing and mTOR regulation

### SGF29 and Chromatin
- **SGF29**: SAGA complex component, recognizes histone H3K4me3
- Potential link between chromatin state and mTOR-regulated transcription
- May coordinate growth signals with epigenetic programs

### PKA Signaling Crosstalk
- **PRKAR1A**: Protein kinase A regulatory subunit 1 alpha
- PKA and mTOR pathways show extensive crosstalk
- Both respond to nutrient and hormonal signals

## Clinical Relevance
- **Cancer**: Hyperactive mTOR in many cancers; rapamycin and rapalogs used as therapeutics
- **Tuberous sclerosis**: TSC1/TSC2 mutations cause mTORC1 hyperactivation
- **Metabolic disorders**: mTOR dysregulation in diabetes and obesity
- **Aging**: mTOR inhibition extends lifespan in model organisms

## Therapeutic Targeting
- **Rapamycin**: Selective mTORC1 inhibitor
- **Rapalogs**: Everolimus, temsirolimus (mTORC1 inhibitors)
- **ATP-competitive inhibitors**: Target both mTORC1 and mTORC2
