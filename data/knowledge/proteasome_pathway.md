# Proteasome-Mediated Protein Degradation

## Overview
The ubiquitin-proteasome system (UPS) is the major pathway for selective protein degradation in eukaryotic cells. It regulates protein quality control, cell cycle progression, signal transduction, and transcription.

## 26S Proteasome Structure

### 20S Core Particle (Catalytic Core)
The 20S proteasome is a barrel-shaped structure with proteolytic activity:

**Alpha Subunits (α1-α7):** Form outer rings, gate access to catalytic chamber
- **PSMA1** (α6): Structural subunit
- **PSMA2** (α2): Structural subunit
- **PSMA3** (α7): Structural subunit
- **PSMA4** (α3): Structural subunit
- **PSMA5** (α5): Structural subunit
- **PSMA6** (α1): Structural subunit
- **PSMA7** (α4): Structural subunit

**Beta Subunits (β1-β7):** Form inner rings, contain catalytic sites
- **PSMB1** (β6): Structural subunit
- **PSMB2** (β4): Structural subunit
- **PSMB3** (β3): Structural subunit
- **PSMB4** (β7): Structural subunit
- **PSMB5** (β5): Chymotrypsin-like activity (primary catalytic site)
- **PSMB6** (β1): Caspase-like activity
- **PSMB7** (β2): Trypsin-like activity

### 19S Regulatory Particle
Recognizes ubiquitinated substrates and unfolds proteins for degradation:

**Base Subcomplex (ATPases):**
- **PSMC1-6** (Rpt1-6): AAA+ ATPases that unfold substrates

**Lid Subcomplex:**
- **PSMD1-14**: Non-ATPase regulatory subunits
- **PSMD14**: Deubiquitinating enzyme, removes ubiquitin chains

### Proteasome Assembly
- **POMP** (Proteasome maturation protein): Chaperone required for 20S assembly
- **PSMG1-4**: Proteasome assembly chaperones
- **PAC1-4**: Proteasome assembly chaperones

## Ubiquitination Machinery

### E1 Ubiquitin-Activating Enzymes
- **UBA1**: Primary E1 enzyme (activates ubiquitin)

### E2 Ubiquitin-Conjugating Enzymes
- **UBE2R2** (CDC34): Cell division cycle 34, involved in SCF complex-mediated ubiquitination
- Transfers ubiquitin from E1 to E3 or substrate

### E3 Ubiquitin Ligases
Provide substrate specificity:
- **SCF complexes**: Skp1-Cullin-F-box protein complexes
- **APC/C**: Anaphase-promoting complex/cyclosome
- **HECT domain E3s**: Direct transfer of ubiquitin to substrate
- **RING domain E3s**: Facilitate E2-substrate interaction

## Regulatory Mechanisms

### NF-κB Pathway Connection
The proteasome is essential for NF-κB activation:
- Degrades IκB inhibitor, releasing NF-κB for nuclear translocation
- **AKIRIN2**: Nuclear factor that facilitates NF-κB-dependent transcription
- AKIRIN2 may coordinate proteasome activity with NF-κB signaling

### Cell Cycle Regulation
- **APC/C**: Ubiquitin ligase that targets cyclins and securin for degradation
- Proteasome-mediated degradation of cyclins drives cell cycle transitions

### DNA Damage Response
- Proteasome degrades damaged or misfolded proteins after genotoxic stress
- Regulates p53 stability and activity

## Novel Connections

### AKIRIN2 and Proteasome Coordination
- **AKIRIN2**: Nuclear protein, conserved from flies to humans
- Originally identified in innate immunity and NF-κB signaling
- May link proteasome activity to transcriptional programs
- Potential role in coordinating protein degradation with gene expression

### UBE2R2 Specificity
- **UBE2R2** (CDC34): Specialized E2 for SCF complexes
- Phosphorylation-dependent regulation
- Critical for G1/S transition through cyclin E degradation

## Clinical Relevance

### Cancer
- **Proteasome inhibitors**: Bortezomib, carfilzomib (FDA-approved for multiple myeloma)
- Cancer cells often dependent on high proteasome activity
- Mutations in proteasome subunits rare but can affect drug sensitivity

### Neurodegenerative Diseases
- Impaired proteasome function linked to:
  - Alzheimer's disease
  - Parkinson's disease
  - Huntington's disease
- Accumulation of misfolded proteins when UPS is overwhelmed

### Immunoproteasome
- **PSMB8, PSMB9, PSMB10**: Inducible catalytic subunits (replace β5, β1, β2)
- Expressed in immune cells and upon IFN-γ stimulation
- Optimized for MHC class I antigen presentation

## Therapeutic Strategies
- **Proteasome inhibitors**: Block protein degradation, induce apoptosis in cancer cells
- **E3 ligase inhibitors**: Target specific degradation pathways
- **PROTACs**: Proteolysis-targeting chimeras, recruit E3 ligases to degrade specific proteins
