# Mitochondrial Homeostasis and Oxidative Phosphorylation

## Overview
Mitochondrial homeostasis encompasses the processes that maintain mitochondrial function, including biogenesis, dynamics (fission/fusion), mitophagy, and oxidative phosphorylation (OXPHOS). The electron transport chain (ETC) and ATP synthesis are central to cellular energy metabolism.

## Electron Transport Chain Complexes

### Complex I (NADH Dehydrogenase)
- **NDUFA13**: Subunit of Complex I, essential for electron transfer
- **NDUFB11**: Accessory subunit required for Complex I assembly
- **NDUFS2**: Core catalytic subunit of Complex I

### Complex III (Cytochrome bc1 Complex)
- **CYC1**: Cytochrome c1, component of Complex III involved in electron transfer to cytochrome c
- **UQCRFS1**: Rieske iron-sulfur protein, critical for Complex III function

### Complex IV (Cytochrome c Oxidase)
- **COX7A2**: Subunit of Complex IV
- **COX7C**: Assembly factor and structural component

### Complex V (ATP Synthase)
- **ATP5MF**: Component of the F1F0 ATP synthase complex
- **ATP5PO**: Mitochondrial ATP synthase subunit

## Mitochondrial Dynamics

### Fission and Fusion
- **DNM1L** (DRP1): Dynamin-related protein that mediates mitochondrial fission
- **MFF**: Mitochondrial fission factor, recruits DRP1 to mitochondria
- **OPA1**: GTPase mediating mitochondrial inner membrane fusion

### Cristae Organization
- **CHCHD3**: Mitochondrial intermembrane space protein involved in cristae maintenance
- **MICOS complex**: Maintains cristae junction architecture

## Novel Roles in Mitochondrial Function

### Oncogenic Drivers with Mitochondrial Connections
- **KRAS**: While primarily known as an oncogene, KRAS mutations drive metabolic reprogramming
  - Mutant KRAS increases OXPHOS dependency in certain cancer contexts
  - KRAS regulates mitochondrial dynamics and mitophagy
  - Links growth signaling to mitochondrial bioenergetics

- **BRAF**: RAF kinase oncogene, unexpected mitochondrial roles
  - BRAF mutations alter cellular metabolism and OXPHOS
  - BRAF can localize to mitochondria and regulate apoptosis
  - Emerging evidence for BRAF in mitochondrial quality control

### Mitochondrial Quality Control
- **PINK1**: Kinase that senses mitochondrial damage and recruits Parkin
- **PRKN** (Parkin): E3 ubiquitin ligase mediating mitophagy
- **USP30**: Deubiquitinase opposing Parkin-mediated mitophagy

## Metabolic Regulation

### TCA Cycle Integration
- **IDH2**: Isocitrate dehydrogenase, produces NADPH in mitochondria
- **SUCLG2**: Succinate-CoA ligase, TCA cycle enzyme
- **MDH2**: Malate dehydrogenase, final step of TCA cycle

### Mitochondrial Biogenesis
- **PGC1A**: Master regulator of mitochondrial biogenesis
- **NRF1/NRF2**: Transcription factors downstream of PGC1A
- **TFAM**: Mitochondrial transcription factor

## Mitochondrial Dysfunction Diseases
- **Leigh syndrome**: Defects in OXPHOS complexes
- **MELAS/MERRF**: Mitochondrial tRNA mutations
- **Parkinson's disease**: PINK1/Parkin mutations impair mitophagy
- **Cancer metabolic reprogramming**: Oncogenes like KRAS/BRAF alter mitochondrial function

## Clinical Relevance
Targeting mitochondrial metabolism is a therapeutic strategy in cancer. KRAS-mutant cancers show variable OXPHOS dependency, making them potential targets for mitochondrial inhibitors.
