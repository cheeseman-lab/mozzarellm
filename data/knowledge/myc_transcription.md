# MYC-Driven Transcriptional Regulation and Chromatin Remodeling

## Overview
MYC is a master transcription factor that controls cell growth, proliferation, and metabolism. MYC regulates thousands of genes by recruiting chromatin remodeling complexes, histone-modifying enzymes, and the transcriptional machinery. Dysregulated MYC drives many cancers.

## MYC Function and Regulation

### MYC as a Transcription Factor
- **MYC**: bHLH-ZIP transcription factor that dimerizes with MAX
- **MAX**: MYC-associated factor X, obligate binding partner
- **MXD1/MXD3/MXD4**: MYC antagonists that compete for MAX binding

### Transcriptional Activation
- MYC binds E-box DNA elements (CACGTG)
- Recruits histone acetyltransferases (HATs) and chromatin remodelers
- Promotes RNA polymerase II recruitment and pause release

## Chromatin Remodeling in MYC-Driven Transcription

### Histone Methylation
- **SETD2**: H3K36me3 methyltransferase with roles in transcription elongation
  - While classically known for H3K36 trimethylation, SETD2 is implicated in MYC target gene regulation
  - SETD2 loss can alter MYC-dependent transcription
  - Emerging evidence suggests SETD2 modulates chromatin accessibility at MYC-bound loci

### SWI/SNF Chromatin Remodeling
- **SMARCA4** (BRG1): ATPase subunit of SWI/SNF complex
- **ARID1A**: DNA-binding subunit of SWI/SNF
- MYC recruits SWI/SNF to target promoters for chromatin opening

### Histone Acetylation
- **GCN5** (KAT2A): HAT recruited by MYC to activate transcription
- **TIP60**: HAT complex involved in MYC-mediated gene activation
- Acetylation marks open chromatin at MYC target genes

## MYC Target Genes and Pathways

### Ribosome Biogenesis
- MYC upregulates ribosomal protein genes (RPL/RPS genes)
- Drives rRNA transcription via Pol I
- Links cell growth to protein synthesis capacity

### Cell Cycle Progression
- **CDK4/CDK6**: Cyclin-dependent kinases regulated by MYC
- **CCND2**: Cyclin D2, MYC target driving G1/S transition
- **E2F**: Transcription factors amplified by MYC

### Metabolism
- **LDHA**: Lactate dehydrogenase A, MYC promotes glycolysis
- **GLS**: Glutaminase, MYC drives glutamine addiction
- **SLC family**: MYC upregulates nutrient transporters

## MYC Repression and Tumor Suppression

### MYC Antagonists
- **MXD proteins**: Repress MYC target genes by recruiting HDACs
- **MIZ1**: Transcription factor repressed when bound by MYC

### Negative Feedback
- **MYC autoregulation**: MYC represses its own promoter
- **p53**: Activated by MYC-induced oncogenic stress

## Epigenetic Readers and Writers

### Bromodomain Proteins
- **BRD4**: Reads acetylated histones at MYC targets, recruits transcription machinery
- **BRD2/BRD3**: Additional bromodomain proteins in MYC regulation
- BET inhibitors block MYC-driven transcription

### Polycomb Repressive Complexes
- **EZH2**: H3K27me3 methyltransferase, represses MYC antagonists
- **SUZ12**: Core subunit of PRC2

## MYC in Cancer

### MYC Amplification
- Burkitt lymphoma: MYC translocation t(8;14)
- Neuroblastoma: MYCN amplification
- Lung, breast, ovarian cancers: MYC amplification common

### Therapeutic Targeting
- **BET inhibitors**: Block BRD4-MYC interaction
- **CDK inhibitors**: Target MYC-driven cell cycle
- **Metabolic inhibitors**: Exploit MYC-induced metabolic dependencies

## Clinical Relevance
MYC is one of the most frequently dysregulated oncogenes. Targeting MYC-dependent chromatin remodeling and transcription is an active area of cancer therapy development.
